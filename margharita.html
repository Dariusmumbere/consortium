Who We Are

Rooted in community,
growing for the world

✅ Reg. No. P.22105/RCS · Cooperative Societies of Uganda

Margharita Organic Vanilla Farmers' Cooperative Society Ltd (MOVFACOS) is a farmer-initiated cooperative started in 2013, formally registered on 07/07/2021. From a small community-based organisation, it has grown to 2,551 members across 25 farmer groups.

Headquartered in Kisanga B, Nyamwamba Division, Kasese Municipality — 200 metres from Nyakasanga Highway — MOVFACOS provides technical support, collective marketing, value-chain processing and capacity building to the rural farming communities of the Rwenzori region.

Governing Structure

Five tiers.
One cooperative.

MOVFACOS is governed through a transparent structure that places members at the centre — from the general assembly down to the communities where services are delivered.

Tier 1 — Membership
General Assembly — the sovereign body of all cooperative members
Tier 2 — Executive
Chairperson, Vice, Secretary, Treasurer & 5 Committee Members
Tier 3 — Sub-Committees
Specialist committees overseeing implementation of society activities
Tier 4 — Administration
Technical management team headed by the Manager — planning & operations
Tier 5 — Communities
25 affiliate community farmer groups — where production and marketing happen

What we are
building together

Our Activities

How we work
on the ground

📦
Procurement & Marketing
Bulk purchase of raw materials — vanilla, cocoa, coffee, maize, groundnuts, soya bean and fish — from farmers at official buying dates with fair pricing.
🎓
Farmer Training
Training in production, postharvest handling, good agronomic practices and record keeping across all value chains.
🌳
Tree Nursery Project

💰
Village Savings & Loans

Value Chain Processing

Youth Vocational Skills

 Current Challenges

Where we need
support to grow

MOVFACOS has identified key gaps that — with the right investment — would unlock significant growth for member farmers.

  • Working capital for timely payment of farmers on delivery
  • Limited high-yielding seed varieties to increase farm-level production
  • Only one tractor — constraining raw material production targets
  • Insufficient storage space for seasonal produce volumes
  • No transport truck for moving raw materials to central stores
  • Need for animal feed processing equipment for poultry and pig farmers
  • Inadequate land for commercial-scale production
